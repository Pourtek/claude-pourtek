## Company Overview

Pourtek is a self-serve beverage tap system built for bars, restaurants, stadiums, hotels, golf courses, grocery stores, universities, residential communities, entertainment venues, airports, food halls, event venues, resorts, and other high-volume beverage environments.

Pourtek helps operators serve drinks faster, reduce waste, lower labor pressure, improve guest experience, and manage beverage programs with better visibility and control.

Customers tap an RFID card, wristband, or supported mobile credential, select a beverage, pour their own drink, and are charged by the ounce.

Pourtek was founded by bar owners to solve real hospitality problems:

- Staff shortages
- Beverage loss
- Long lines
- Slow service
- Missed sales
- Poor inventory visibility
- Overpouring
- Free drinks
- High labor costs
- Limited reporting

## Core Products

### Tap Walls

Tap Walls are fixed self-serve tap installations built into or along bar and venue walls.

Tap Walls are best for:

- Bars
- Restaurants
- Breweries
- Stadiums
- Food halls
- Universities
- High-volume permanent beverage areas
- Multi-tap beverage programs

Tap Walls are customizable and can be built with as many faucets as the venue wants, based on layout, beverage program, and installation needs.

Tap Wall rule:

- Tap Walls always use 2 faucets per customer-facing screen.

Examples:

- 2 wall taps = 1 screen
- 4 wall taps = 2 screens
- 6 wall taps = 3 screens
- 8 wall taps = 4 screens
- 10 wall taps = 5 screens
- Larger custom Tap Walls follow the same 2 faucets per screen rule

Positioning:

Tap Walls are customizable fixed installations for venues that want a permanent self-serve beverage wall.

- Suspending customers

Positioning:

## How Pourtek Works

Basic customer flow:

1. Staff creates a tab using the handheld, POS integration, RFID card, wristband, or supported customer credential.
2. Customer taps their RFID card, wristband, or supported credential at the tap.
3. The screen validates the customer and activates beverage selection.
4. Customer selects a beverage when needed.
5. Customer pours their own drink.
6. Pourtek tracks ounces, servings, and cost in real time.
7. The customer’s tab updates automatically.
8. Staff can monitor activity, manage limits, change kegs, and review reporting.

Simple explanation:

Customers tap, pour, and pay by the ounce. Operators track every pour.

## RFID Explanation

RFID stands for Radio-Frequency Identification.

In Pourtek, RFID is the technology inside the card or wristband customers tap to activate a faucet.

RFID connects the customer’s tab to their right to pour.

Use simple language when explaining RFID:

“Guests tap their RFID card or wristband, pour what they want, and Pourtek tracks the exact amount.”

Avoid making RFID sound overly technical unless the user asks for technical detail.

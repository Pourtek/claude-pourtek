## Screens, Handhelds, Cloud Platform & Technical Differentiators

### Customer-Facing Screens

Pourtek’s customer-facing screens are the guest’s main interaction point before and during a pour.

The customer-facing screen is mounted above or near the taps and is designed to attract, inform, and guide the customer through the self-pour experience.

Known screen detail:

- Example screen size: 10.1-inch widescreen display
- Mounted above the taps
- Used to show beverage visuals, branding, price per ounce, ABV, style, keg level, pour information, and customer prompts

Important note:

Use “10.1-inch widescreen” as the known example size unless the user confirms that all current screens are exactly this size.

### What the Guest Screen Shows

The customer-facing screen can show:

- Beverage name
- Beverage image or can visual
- Price per ounce
- ABV
- Beverage style
- Beverage description
- Keg level indicator
- Pour page
- Ounces poured in real time
- Running cost in real time
- Pour summary after the pour
- Custom branded backgrounds
- Event graphics
- Animated overlays
- Color profiles
- Interactive visual elements

### Guest Screen Pour Flow

The customer flow is simple:

1. Customer taps their RFID card or wristband.
2. The system validates the customer’s active tab.
3. Customer selects a beverage on the screen.
4. The screen shifts to the pour page.
5. Customer pulls the tap handle.
6. Screen displays ounces poured and cost in real time.
7. When the customer releases the handle, the pour stops.
8. A summary screen displays total ounces and total cost before returning to idle.

If a customer taps their RFID but does not select a beverage within the timeout window, the session automatically ends and the tap becomes available again.

### Keg Level Indicator

The guest screen includes a visual keg level indicator.

This appears as a colored ring or perimeter around the beverage icon.

Typical status colors:

- Green: full or high keg level
- Yellow: medium or lower keg level
- Red: low keg level

This lets customers see keg status before they tap their card or wristband.

Staff can also view keg level information from the handheld, usually with more precise percentage or volume detail.

## Staff Handheld System

### Android Handheld Command Center

Pourtek is managed through Android handheld devices.

The handheld is the staff command center for the self-pour operation.

It helps eliminate the need for desktop POS hardware at the self-pour point of operation.

The handheld can support:

- Creating customer tabs
- Managing customer tabs
- Scanning driver’s licenses
- Verifying age
- Assigning RFID cards or wristbands
- Reading RFID
- Pairing RFID credentials
- Printing receipts
- Monitoring active faucets
- Authorizing customers
- Re-authorizing customers after consumption limits
- Suspending customer RFID access
- Changing kegs
- Updating inventory
- Running reports
- Managing faucet status
- Disabling individual faucets
- Disabling all faucets
- Supporting staff from anywhere in the venue

### Built-In Handheld Hardware

The Android handheld may include:

- Built-in receipt printer
- Barcode scanner for driver’s licenses
- NFC reader for RFID setup
- RFID scanner or RFID-reading capability
- Android touch interface

Use this positioning:

“Staff can manage the self-pour operation from a handheld instead of being tied to a fixed desktop station.”

### Staff Handheld Gestures

The handheld uses familiar Android gestures such as:

- Tap
- Swipe
- Long-hold

This helps make staff onboarding faster and more intuitive.

### Customer Tab Creation Methods

The handheld supports multiple ways to create or manage a customer tab:

- Driver’s license scan
- Quick Tab
- Existing RFID or VIP scan
- Manual entry
- QR code or mobile wallet workflow where supported or in rollout

Tab types may include:

- Open Tab
- Cash Tab / prepaid tab
- Permanent RFID / VIP RFID

### Open Tab

An Open Tab works like a traditional bar tab.

Charges accumulate and are settled at the end.

It can be linked to a credit card and still remains subject to ounce or serving limits.

### Cash Tab / Prepaid Tab

A Cash Tab is a prepaid balance.

The customer can pour until the balance is depleted.

The system blocks additional pours unless staff adds more funds.

### Permanent RFID

Permanent RFID can be used for VIPs, members, or returning customers who keep the same RFID credential across visits.

### Mobile Wallet / Tap-to-Go

Current systems use RFID cards or wristbands to activate faucets.

A newer or rollout feature allows users to receive a wallet pass on Android or Apple phones for tap-to-go functionality.

Use cautious language:

“Supported mobile credential workflows may allow guests to use a phone-based pass instead of a physical RFID card or wristband.”

Do not overstate this as universally available unless the user confirms rollout status.

## RFID & Customer Activation

RFID stands for Radio-Frequency Identification.

In Pourtek, RFID is the technology inside the card or wristband customers tap to activate a faucet.

RFID connects the customer’s tab to their right to pour.

Simple explanation:

“Guests tap their RFID card or wristband, select a beverage, pour what they want, and Pourtek tracks the exact amount.”

Customer activation flow:

1. Staff creates or opens a customer tab.
2. Staff assigns an RFID card or wristband.
3. Customer taps RFID at the screen.
4. System validates the tab.
5. Customer selects and pours.
6. Pourtek tracks volume and cost in real time.
7. The customer’s tab updates automatically.

## Consumption Limits & Responsible-Service Controls

Pourtek supports responsible-service workflows through configurable consumption limits.

The system can track:

- Ounces
- Servings
- ABV-aware serving logic
- THC beverage consumption limits where legal and appropriate
- Customer tab status
- Customer authorization status

Default limits may be applied to new tabs, such as ounce limits or serving limits.

When a customer reaches their limit:

1. The system blocks additional pouring.
2. The screen displays a limit message.
3. Staff receives a visual alert on the handheld.
4. The customer must see an attendant.
5. Staff can re-authorize additional ounces if appropriate.
6. Staff can also suspend the customer’s RFID access.

Staff status cues may include:

- Blinking customer name when attention is needed
- Color-coded status after re-authorization
- Suspended status when access is disabled

External-safe phrasing:

“Pourtek supports responsible-service workflows with configurable limits, staff alerts, re-authorization, and RFID suspension tools.”

Avoid saying:

- “Guarantees compliance”
- “Prevents overserving”
- “Eliminates liability”

## POS & GoTab Integration

For venues using GoTab, Pourtek can integrate rather than replace the existing POS workflow.

GoTab workflow:

1. Staff opens a customer tab in GoTab.
2. Staff uses an external RFID reader connected to the GoTab terminal.
3. Staff swipes or pairs an RFID pass with the customer tab.
4. Pourtek validates the customer through the GoTab API.
5. The system checks for an open tab.
6. The system respects consumption limits.

External-safe phrasing:

“Pourtek can integrate with GoTab-supported workflows by pairing RFID credentials to open customer tabs.”

Avoid saying Pourtek integrates with every POS unless confirmed.

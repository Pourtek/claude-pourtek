# Pourlytics Dashboard & Inventory Guide

## Purpose

This file explains what each major metric, chart, table, and insight means inside the Pourlytics dashboard and inventory management screens.

Use this as Claude project knowledge for creating PourTek marketing content, sales enablement material, one-pagers, landing pages, discovery questions, product explainers, and operator-facing dashboard descriptions.

---

# Overview Dashboard

The Overview dashboard shows operators where revenue is coming from, what is being poured, customer activity, margin performance, keg health, and AI-driven recommendations.

## Top Navigation

### Overview / Sales / Inventory / Reports
These are the main software sections.

- **Overview**: High-level business performance.
- **Sales**: Revenue, transactions, pours, and product-level sales data.
- **Inventory**: Keg status, restocking, low inventory, empty kegs, and available backups.
- **Reports**: Exportable or deeper reporting views.

### LIVE / Updated Time
Shows that dashboard data is live or recently refreshed.

Example:
- **LIVE**
- **Updated 6:05 PM**

This tells the operator the dashboard is current enough to support real-time decisions.

### Date Range
The selected date range controls the data shown across the dashboard.

Example:
- **6/11/2026 - 6/18/2026**
- **Last 7 Days**

All revenue, pour, customer, and inventory metrics shown are based on this selected window.

# Top KPI Cards

## Total Charged

### Meaning
The total revenue charged through the self-pour system during the selected date range.

### Example
**$7,907.84**

### Why it matters
Shows total sales generated by the system.

### Prior Period Comparison
Example:

# Sales & Pours Over Time

## Meaning
This chart compares sales activity, pour volume, and customer activity over time.

## Chart Elements

### Green Bars: Total Charged
Revenue generated for each day or hour.

### Blue Bars: Oz Poured
Total beverage volume poured for each day or hour.

### Orange Line: Customers
Customer count over the same time period.

## Why it matters
This helps operators understand:
- Which days or hours generate the most revenue.
- Which days or hours have the highest pour volume.
- Whether customer traffic matches revenue.
- Whether customers are pouring more or spending more on specific days.
- Staffing, promo, and event timing opportunities.

## Example Insight
If June 12 shows the highest revenue, highest ounces poured, and highest customer count, that was the strongest day in the selected period.

# Keg Health Overview

## Meaning
Summarizes the status of all tracked kegs.

## Example
**111 Total Kegs**

## Status Types

### Assigned
Kegs currently assigned to taps.

Example:
**80 Assigned**

### Available
Backup kegs available in inventory.

Example:
**14 Available**

### Empty
Kegs that are empty and need to be swapped, returned, credited, or reordered.

Example:
**17 Empty**

## Why it matters
Gives operators a fast view of current inventory health.

---

# Keg Forecast

## Meaning
Predicts when active kegs will run out based on recent pour activity.

## Columns

### Beverage
The beverage name.

### % Remaining
How much of the keg is left.

### Days Until Empty
Estimated number of days before the keg runs out.

## Example
**Franciscan Cabernet**
- **3.9% remaining**
- **3.8 days until empty**

## Why it matters
Helps operators prevent stockouts before they happen.

This is useful for:
- Scheduling keg swaps.
- Placing reorders.
- Preparing for busy days.
- Avoiding disappointed customers.
- Keeping top sellers available.

---

# Slow Movers

## Meaning
Shows old or slow-selling kegs.

## Columns

### Beverage
The beverage name.

### Age Days
How long the keg has been sitting.

## Example

- Remove unpopular items.

# Restock Forecast by Category

## Meaning
Shows reorder needs by beverage category.

## Fields

### Projected Empty
How many kegs in that category are expected to run out soon.

### Available
How many backup kegs are already available in that category.

### Net to Order
How many kegs should be ordered after accounting for available backups.

## Example

### Cider
- Projected Empty: **2**
- Available: **0**
- Net to Order: **2**

Meaning:
The operator should order 2 cider kegs.

### Light
- Projected Empty: **4**
- Available: **8**
- Net to Order: **0**

Meaning:
No reorder is needed because available backups cover expected demand.

## Why it matters
This section turns inventory data into a clear ordering recommendation.

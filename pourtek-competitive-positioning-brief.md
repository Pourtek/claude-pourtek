# PourTek Competitive Positioning Brief

## Purpose

This document guides PourTek marketing, website, landing page, sales collateral, and design work. The goal is to make PourTek feel more polished, premium, trustworthy, and operator-focused while avoiding the overbuilt, loud, or generic feel of larger self-pour competitors.

## Current Brand Opportunity

PourTek has strong product potential but should avoid feeling small, unfinished, or founder-led in public-facing materials. The brand should present itself as a modern, credible self-pour beverage system built for operators who care about design, workflow, guest experience, and return.

PourTek should own a cleaner position in the category:

**Best-designed self-pour systems for modern operators.**

## Competitive Takeaways

### PourMyBeer

PourMyBeer feels aggressive, ROI-heavy, and sales-forward. It uses strong proof, profit claims, and repeated CTAs. PourTek should learn from the proof and ROI structure but avoid sounding overly loud or pushy.

### iPourIt

iPourIt feels more polished, technical, and enterprise-ready. It uses structured content, proof, platform language, and operational detail. PourTek should learn from the polish and structure but avoid becoming too corporate or cold.

### PourTek Opportunity

PourTek should feel more stylish, flexible, approachable, and design-forward. The brand can win by combining clean design, obvious operator value, and a simpler buying experience.

## Core Positioning

**Headline direction:**
Self-Pour Systems Built to Look Better, Work Harder, and Sell More.

**Sub-headline direction:**
Modern beverage tech for operators who want a cleaner guest experience, simpler operations, and a stronger return.

**Value proposition:**
PourTek helps operators launch self-pour with sharper design, smarter workflow, and a more premium guest experience.

## Brand Personality

PourTek should feel:

* Modern
* Premium
* Clean
* Operator-first
* Confident
* Practical
* Flexible
* Hospitality-aware
* Less corporate than enterprise competitors
* More polished than a small vendor

Avoid sounding:

* Cheap
* Overly technical
* Too startup-like
* Too salesy
* Generic
* Cluttered
* Over-explained

## Visual Direction

Design should feel clean, premium, and conversion-focused.

Use:

* Strong hero sections
* Clear headlines
* High-quality product-in-context visuals
* Real venue imagery when possible
* Clean spacing
* Simple section hierarchy
* Product callouts
* Operator-focused proof points
* Premium but approachable layouts

Avoid:

* Overcrowded sections
* Stock-looking visuals
* Random icons with no purpose
* Too many competing CTAs
* Generic hospitality imagery
* Dense blocks of text
* Overly shiny or unrealistic product renders

## Website and Landing Page Rules

Every important page should answer these questions quickly:

1. What is PourTek?
2. Who is it for?
3. What problem does it solve?
4. Why is it better?
5. What does the setup look like?
6. What should the visitor do next?

Recommended page sections:

1. Hero with clear category statement
2. Operator problem
3. PourTek solution
4. Product options: tap walls, Tekerators, dashboard
5. How it works in 3 simple steps
6. Use cases by vertical
7. Proof points and outcomes
8. Installation or layout flexibility
9. CTA section

## Proof Points to Use

Use these proof points where relevant:

* 70+ installs
* Increased beverage sales
* Reduced waste
* Lower labor pressure
* RFID-controlled self-pour
* Real-time dashboard
* Mobile and flexible setup options
* Tap walls and portable Tekerators
* Built for beer, wine, cocktails, coffee, kombucha, and other beverages
* Financing available when relevant

## Preferred CTAs

Use clear CTAs, but keep them polished and not desperate.

Recommended:

* Book a Demo
* Plan Your Setup
* See the Setup
* Build Your Pour Plan
* Talk Through Your Space
* Explore Tekerators
* Get a Layout Review

Avoid:

* Buy Now
* Start Pouring Profits Today
* Ready to Tap Into Profits?
* Overly aggressive demo language

## Vertical Positioning

### Indoor Golf Facilities

Focus on flexible beverage service around simulator bays, league nights, lounges, patios, and events.

Possible language:
Self-pour that plays through your space.

### Pickleball Facilities

Focus on keeping guests on-site longer, reducing bar congestion, and supporting social play.

Possible language:
A smarter beverage setup for the pace of modern pickleball.

### Golf Courses

Focus on clubhouse service, patios, simulator areas, outings, tournaments, and the 19th hole.

Possible language:
A better beverage experience from the first tee to the 19th hole.

## Tekerator Messaging

Tekerators should be positioned as flexible, portable self-pour stations that let operators add beverage service without building around a fixed tap wall.

Key message:
Tekerators let operators build self-pour around the space they already have.

Use cases:

* Simulator bays
* Clubhouses
* Patios
* Event rooms
* Mobile bar areas
* Pop-ups
* Private events
* Overflow beverage service

## Design Principle

PourTek should look like the premium, modern choice in self-pour, not the loudest vendor in the category.

The brand should make buyers feel:

* This is easier than I thought.
* This will look good in my space.
* This can help my staff.
* This can make money.
* This company knows how operators actually work.
